PROJECT 01 / STRUCTURAL DESIGN

Lightweight aircraft-style structural bracket

FEA-driven design optimised for stiffness at minimum mass, validated on an Instron rig.

The brief was to design a bracket that met defined stiffness targets under both tension and compression at the lowest possible mass. I modelled it in Inventor and ran iterative ANSYS FEA to see where material was earning its place and where it was not, checking the results against hand calculations. I then programmed the CNC toolpaths to machine it from polycarbonate sheet, designing the geometry to be machinable within the available tooling, and verified the finished part on an Instron tensile and compression test.

PROJECT 02 / MECHANISM DESIGN

Multi-mechanism ball-transport device

A timed transport mechanism designed to a precise 5.0 second specification.

This device had to move a ball through a sequence of mechanisms and hit a fixed timing target of 5.0 seconds. I used Autodesk Inventor's dynamic simulation to tune the geometry and motion before committing to anything physical, then prototyped the parts through 3D printing and laser cutting. Getting the timing to land reliably meant iterating until the simulation and the real build agreed.

PROJECT 03 / MECHATRONICS

Arduino-driven task robot

Design lead (CAD) for a team robot built to pick up and release a ball.

As the design lead on a team robotics project, I designed the laser-cut and 3D-printed body and the ball pick-up and release mechanisms, and worked on the embedded Arduino integration. It was a good exercise in taking a system from CAD and code through to a working physical machine, and in making the mechanical design and the control side fit together.

PROJECT 04 / DESIGN & MANUFACTURE

Injection-moulded case & gear reducer study

Two design-for-manufacture exercises: a moulded silicone part and a full reducer design.

I designed and injection-moulded a silicone phone case using 3D-printed mould tooling, working through the practicalities of designing a part around the way it would actually be made. Separately, I fully documented a gear reducer design, sizing and specifying the shafts, gears, bearings, keys, and the clutch and brake. Together these built up my design-for-manufacture thinking from two quite different angles.
